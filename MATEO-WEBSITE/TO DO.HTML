What to do?



What should I do if someone is having a Heart Attack? If you think someone someone is having a heart attack, act fast — every second counts.

Home Return Proceed

First, call emergency services immediately. In the Philippines, dial 911 or your local emergency number. Don’t wait to see if the symptoms go away — early treatment can save their life. While waiting for help to arrive, keep the person calm and seated. Don’t let them walk around or lie flat. Sitting upright or slightly reclined is usually best, as it helps ease the strain on the heart.

Ask if they have chest pain medication, such as nitroglycerin, and help them take it if prescribed. If not allergic and they’re conscious, you may offer them a regular aspirin (325 mg) to chew slowly. Aspirin helps thin the blood and may reduce heart damage — but only if they’re not allergic, not bleeding, and not advised against it by a doctor.

If the person becomes unconscious and is not breathing, begin CPR (cardiopulmonary resuscitation) immediately. Push hard and fast in the center of the chest — about 100 to 120 compressions per minute — and continue until emergency help arrives. If an automated external defibrillator (AED) is available, use it as instructed.


Reminder from Heart & Soul Co. A heart attack is not the time to panic — it's the time to act. You don’t have to be a medical expert to save a life. Knowing the signs and what to do in those first few minutes can make the difference between survival and tragedy. Stay calm, stay informed, and be ready to help.
