What is a Heart Attack?



A heart attack, also known as myocardial infarction, happens when the blood flow to a part of the heart muscle is blocked — most often by a blood clot. Without blood and oxygen, the affected part of the heart begins to die. This is a medical emergency that requires immediate attention.

Heart attack symptoms include chest pain, pressure, and discomfort that can spread to the arms, neck, jaw, back, or stomach. Shortness of breath is common, along with cold sweat, nausea, lightheadedness, and overwhelming anxiety.

Symptoms may be more subtle in women, older adults, and people with diabetes, such as unusual fatigue or mild back or jaw pain. Some heart attacks are even "silent," causing little to no noticeable pain but still causing significant heart damage.

A heart attack occurs when one or more arteries that supply blood to your heart muscle become suddenly blocked, usually due to a buildup of fat, cholesterol, and other substances (plaque). When the plaque ruptures, a blood clot forms and cuts off blood flow. If this blockage isn’t treated quickly, the heart muscle begins to die. The longer the heart is deprived of oxygen, the greater the damage.



Causes & Risk Factors:
• High blood pressure (Hypertension)
• High cholesterol
• Smoking
• Diabetes
• Obesity
• Sedentary lifestyle
• Family history of heart disease
• Stress and poor diet
